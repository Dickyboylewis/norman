FANATICAL PROSPECTING — KEY CONCEPTS (Jeb Blount)
===================================================

THE 30-DAY RULE
The prospecting you do in any 30-day period will pay off for the next 90 days. If you stop prospecting today, you will feel the pain 30-90 days from now when your pipeline dries up. Never stop prospecting, even when you are busy with active projects.

THE LAW OF REPLACEMENT  
You must always be adding new prospects to your pipeline to replace the ones that naturally fall out. Prospects go cold, deals fall through, contacts change jobs. If you are not constantly filling the top of the funnel, the bottom will dry up.

THE PROSPECTING PYRAMID
Use multiple channels in order of effectiveness:
1. Telephone (most effective for B2B)
2. In-person visits and networking
3. Email
4. Social selling (LinkedIn)
5. Text messaging
6. Referrals
7. Networking events
8. Cold calling

THE THREE Ps
- Proactive: Do not wait for the phone to ring. Go out and make things happen.
- Persistent: Follow up relentlessly. Most deals are won between the 5th and 12th touch.
- Professional: Be respectful, prepared, and always add value.

GOLDEN HOURS
Block your highest-energy hours (usually morning) exclusively for prospecting activity. No email, no admin, no meetings. Just outreach. Protect this time ruthlessly.

THE FANATICAL PROSPECTING FRAMEWORK
1. Define your ideal prospect profile
2. Build targeted lists  
3. Block dedicated prospecting time
4. Use a multi-channel approach
5. Track your activity numbers
6. Review and adjust weekly

RBOs — REFLEX RESPONSES, BRUSH-OFFS, AND OBJECTIONS
- Reflex Responses: Automatic knee-jerk reactions like "I'm busy" or "Send me an email." These are not real objections. Push past them with a simple ledge like "I understand, and that's exactly why I'm calling..."
- Brush-Offs: Slightly more considered but still dismissive. "We already have an architect." Handle by asking a question that opens dialogue.
- Real Objections: Genuine concerns about budget, timing, or fit. These require listening, empathy, and a thoughtful response.

THE OBJECTION HANDLING FRAMEWORK
1. Listen fully — do not interrupt
2. Relate — show empathy ("I understand, a lot of our clients felt the same way initially...")
3. Bridge — connect to a relevant benefit or case study
4. Ask — close with a specific next step ("Would it make sense to have a quick 15-minute chat next Tuesday?")

EMOTIONAL DISCIPLINE
Rejection is not personal. It is a numbers game. The more noes you collect, the closer you are to a yes. Separate your self-worth from the outcome of any single call or email. Focus on activity, not results. Results are a lagging indicator of effort.

KEY METRICS TO TRACK
- Number of outbound calls per day
- Number of emails sent per day
- Number of new contacts added to pipeline per week
- Number of appointments booked per week
- Conversion rate from contact to appointment
- Conversion rate from appointment to proposal
- Conversion rate from proposal to won deal
